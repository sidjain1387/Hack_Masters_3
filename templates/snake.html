SNAKE BITE TREATMENT

Stay calm, we'll take care of everything. You're stronger than you think, and you'll overcome this!!!

FIRST AID MEASURES

1. Stay Calm: Panicking can increase heart rate and spread venom faster through the body. Try to keep the victim as calm as possible.

2. Keep the Affected Area Immobilized: Immobilize the bitten limb or area and keep it at or below the level of the heart. This helps slow the spread of venom.

3. Remove Jewelry and Tight Clothing: Remove any jewelry or tight clothing near the bite site to prevent constriction if swelling occurs.

4. Clean the Bite Area: Gently wash the bite area with soap and water to prevent infection.

5. Apply a Bandage: Apply a clean, dry bandage to the bite, wrapping it snugly but not too tight, to help slow venom spread. Do not use a tourniquet.

6. Seek Medical Help: Get medical assistance as soon as possible, either by calling emergency services or taking the victim to the nearest hospital or medical facility. Time is crucial in treating snake bites.

7. Note the Appearance of the Snake: If possible, try to remember the color, size, and shape of the snake, as this information can help medical professionals determine the appropriate treatment.

8. Avoid Certain Actions: Do not attempt to suck out the venom, make incisions, or apply ice or heat to the bite area, as these methods can worsen the situation.
