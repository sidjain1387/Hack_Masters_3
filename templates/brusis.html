BRUISE TREATMENT

Stay calm, we'll take care of everything. You're stronger than you think, and you'll overcome this!!!

FIRST AID MEASURES

1. Ice Pack: Apply an ice pack wrapped in a cloth to the bruised area for 15-20 minutes. This helps reduce swelling and pain.

2. Elevation: Elevate the bruised area above the level of the heart, if possible. This can also help reduce swelling.

3. Compression: Use a compression bandage or elastic wrap to apply gentle pressure to the bruised area. This can help reduce swelling and provide support.

4. Rest: Avoid putting weight or pressure on the bruised area. Resting the affected limb or body part can aid in healing.

5. Pain Relief: Over-the-counter pain relievers such as ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin) or acetaminophen (Tylenol) can help alleviate pain associated with the bruise.

6. Arnica Gel or Cream: Applying arnica gel or cream to the bruised area may help reduce pain, inflammation, and discoloration.

7. Heat Therapy: After the first 48 hours, applying a warm compress or taking a warm bath may help increase blood circulation and promote healing.

8. Gentle Massage: Once the bruise has started to heal, gently massaging the area can help break up blood clots and improve circulation.

9. Protective Padding: If the bruised area is prone to further injury, such as on the elbows or knees, consider using protective padding or bandages.

10. Seek Medical Attention: If the bruise is severe, accompanied by intense pain, or does not improve within a few days, it's advisable to seek medical attention. Additionally, if there are signs of infection (e.g., increasing pain, redness, warmth, or pus), medical attention should be sought promptly.
