Healthcare Myths vs Facts

Myth: Rubbing alcohol speeds wound healing.

Fact: Use mild soap and water for faster, safer wound care.

Myth: Butter helps with burns.

Fact: Cool water and sterile dressing are better for burn care.

Myth: Tilting head back stops nosebleed.

Fact: Lean forward, pinch nose to prevent throat blood flow.

Myth: Urine soothes jellyfish stings

Fact: Rinse with vinegar or seawater; urine can worsen stings.

Myth: Heat aids sprain healing

Fact: Ice first 48 hours to reduce swelling, then use heat.

Myth: Suck out snake venom.

Fact: Immobilize, seek medical help; sucking can cause infection.

Myth: Coffee or alcohol speeds sobriety..

Fact: Time is the only way to sober up.

Myth: Spoon prevents swallowing tongue during seizure.

Fact: Never put anything in mouth; place person on side.

Myth: Holding breath cures hiccups.

Fact: Try drinking water or breathing into a paper bag.

Myth: Ice directly on sprained ankle reduces swelling.

Fact: Ice directly on sprained ankle reduces swelling.
