ORAL HYGIENE MEASURES

1. Brush twice daily, morning and night, with fluoride toothpaste.

2. Floss once a day to remove plaque between teeth.

3. Use an antimicrobial mouthwash to reduce bacteria and freshen breath.

4. Change toothbrush every 3-4 months or sooner if bristles fray.

5. Avoid tobacco; it stains teeth and causes gum disease.

6. Limit sugary snacks; opt for nutritious, tooth-friendly foods.

7. Stay hydrated; water helps wash away bacteria and food particles.

8. Chew sugar-free gum to stimulate saliva and prevent dry mouth.

9. Visit the dentist for regular check-ups and cleanings twice annually.

10. Replace toothbrush after illness to prevent bacterial reinfection.

11. Clean your tongue with a tongue scraper to remove bacteria.

12. Choose a soft-bristled toothbrush to avoid enamel damage.

13. Don't use teeth as tools; avoid opening packages or biting objects.

14. Consider fluoride treatments to strengthen teeth and prevent decay.

15. Wear a mouthguard for sports to protect teeth from injury.

16. Address teeth grinding; use a night guard to prevent damage.

17. Maintain a balanced diet for overall oral and systemic health.

18. Avoid excessive alcohol; it can contribute to gum disease development.
