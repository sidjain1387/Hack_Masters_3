CUTS & SCRATCHES TREATMENT

Stay calm, we'll take care of everything. You're stronger than you think, and you'll overcome this!!!

FIRST AID MEASURES

1. Stop the Bleeding: Apply direct pressure to the wound using a clean cloth or sterile bandage. Maintain pressure until bleeding stops. Elevate the injured area if possible to reduce blood flow.

2. Clean the Wound: Gently clean the cut or scratch with mild soap and water to remove dirt and debris. Avoid using hydrogen peroxide or alcohol, as they can damage tissue.

3. Apply an Antiseptic: After cleaning, apply an antiseptic solution or ointment to the wound to help prevent infection. Examples include povidone-iodine solution or bacitracin ointment.

4. Cover the Wound: Cover the cleaned wound with a sterile adhesive bandage or gauze pad to protect it from further injury and contamination. Change the dressing daily or as needed.

5. Apply Pressure Points: For larger wounds or deep cuts, apply pressure to pressure points proximal (closer to the body) to the injury site to help control bleeding until medical help arrives.

6. Use Butterfly Bandages: For small cuts or lacerations that are not deep, consider using butterfly bandages to close the wound edges. Apply the adhesive strips across the wound, pulling the skin together.

7. Seek Medical Attention: Seek medical attention if the cut is deep, jagged, or gaping, if it does not stop bleeding after applying pressure, or if there is a risk of infection (e.g., from a dirty object or animal bite).

8. Watch for Signs of Infection: Monitor the wound for signs of infection, such as increased pain, redness, warmth, swelling, or pus. Seek medical attention if any signs of infection develop.

9. Protect the Wound: Keep the wound clean and dry while it heals. Avoid exposing it to dirty or contaminated environments. Consider covering it with a waterproof bandage or dressing if necessary.

10. Update Tetanus Vaccination: If the wound is deep, dirty, or caused by a puncture, ensure that your tetanus vaccination is up to date. Tetanus booster shots are recommended every 10 years or after certain types of injuries.
